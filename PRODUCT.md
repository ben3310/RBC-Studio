# RareBagClub — Product Context

## Product Purpose

RareBagClub is a luxury vintage handbag archive and marketplace. Every piece is sourced, authenticated through a two-stage verification process, and listed with full provenance documentation. Each bag is one-of-one — no restocks, no mass inventory. The experience is closer to acquiring from a private archive or auction house than a traditional e-commerce store.

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary buyer:** Collector-minded women, 28-45. Affluent. They buy for status, investment value, and the story behind an object. They know their houses — Hermès, Chanel, Louis Vuitton, Bottega Veneta. They have researched before landing. Skeptical of fakes. Respond to specificity (blind stamp X, box calfskin, circa 1994) over generic luxury adjectives.

**Secondary buyer:** Quiet luxury aesthetic buyer. Drawn to the editorial feel, the language of "the archive," the idea that this bag has a history. May not be a seasoned collector but aspires to that identity.

**Anti-user:** Fast-fashion shopper, bargain hunter, anyone expecting instant shipping or high-volume SKU variety.

## Brand Voice

- Editorial and precise. Museum catalog meets private members' club.
- Lowercase for poetic copy ("rare pieces. real stories."), title case for functional labels ("REQUEST ACQUISITION").
- No exclamation points. No hyperbole. Specificity is the luxury signal.
- "The Archive" is the conceptual frame. Pieces are "acquired," not "purchased." Customers are "collectors."
- Anti-pattern: generic luxury words (premium, exquisite, finest). Use specific sensory and provenance details instead.

## Strategic Principles

1. **Authenticity signals beat marketing claims.** Trust is built through specificity (archive numbers, provenance cards, archivist signatures) not adjectives.
2. **Scarcity is real.** One of each product. "Last piece in the archive" is always true. Never fake urgency.
3. **Friction reduction at acquisition moment.** Sticky ATC, trust signals under button, urgency above button — all serve the moment of decision.
4. **Editorial gravity.** Page weight and white space should feel like a gallery, not a storefront.
5. **No dark backgrounds.** Cream (#F5F1EC) or warm sand (#EBE7E1) cards only. The archive is lit, not shadowed. **Exception: "After Dark" evening-drop treatment (Noir template).** A dark overlay is sanctioned only for that one named collection, signalling a nighttime drop rather than the default archive mood — it must stay a deliberate, labelled exception, not spread into other templates.

## Platform

Shopify (Horizon theme base, heavily customized). Custom sections prefixed `rbc-*` and `ss-*`. CSS in `rbc-global.css` and section-level `<style>` blocks.

## Anti-References

- Do not mimic Farfetch (too marketplace-y)
- Do not mimic Net-a-Porter (too editorial-magazine, not collector-archive)
- Do not mimic The RealReal (too utilitarian/resale)
- Reference feel: Christie's, 1stDibs private dealer pages, Artsy auction interface
